# NoteAnchor Product Positioning Draft

## One-sentence pitch

1. NoteAnchor is a local desktop tool for attaching notes to exact text fragments without changing the original document.
2. NoteAnchor helps readers and editors keep comments connected to the precise words, phrases, and sentences they refer to.
3. NoteAnchor turns active reading into a structured note-taking workflow by keeping contextual notes beside the source text.

## Short description

NoteAnchor is a local desktop app for active reading and contextual note-taking. It lets you select an exact word, phrase, or sentence in a document, attach a note to it, review all notes later, and export notes or save a print-ready report without modifying the original file.

## Core problem

Notes lose value when they are separated from the exact text they refer to. A comment such as "too vague," "check this source," "better option here," or "important phrase" is only useful when it remains connected to the specific fragment that triggered it. Once notes are moved into a separate document, notebook, or memory, much of that precision is lost.

## Core value

NoteAnchor keeps notes attached to exact text fragments while leaving the original file unchanged. The user can read naturally, mark a precise passage, add a contextual note, and come back later to see both the note and the text that gave it meaning.

## Product principles

- Usefulness first
- No deception
- No exaggerated promises
- No pretending the app does things it does not do
- Original files are not modified
- Describe NoteAnchor honestly and plainly

## Primary audiences

### Editors and proofreaders

- Who they are: people reviewing drafts, manuscripts, articles, or working texts and marking issues for later revision.
- How they use NoteAnchor: they select exact passages and attach comments about clarity, structure, repetition, tone, or possible corrections.
- Why contextual notes help them: the note remains tied to the exact phrase, so revision comments are easier to understand and less likely to become ambiguous later.

### Researchers and analysts

- Who they are: people reading source materials, reports, articles, interview transcripts, or reference texts and extracting observations.
- How they use NoteAnchor: they mark relevant statements, patterns, claims, or evidence and attach short analytical notes while reading.
- Why contextual notes help them: observations remain anchored to the original passage, which makes later review, comparison, and citation checking easier.

### Writers and authors

- Who they are: people reviewing their own drafts, source reading, or reference material while developing ideas.
- How they use NoteAnchor: they mark strong lines, weak passages, revision ideas, or possible structural changes without editing the source immediately.
- Why contextual notes help them: ideas stay connected to the text that triggered them, which supports a cleaner revision workflow and better recall.

### Literary translators

- Who they are: translators working with style, voice, tone, ambiguity, rhythm, and difficult phrasing in literary texts.
- How they use NoteAnchor: they collect difficult fragments, possible alternatives, interpretive questions, and stylistic options directly beside the original sentence.
- Why contextual notes help them: translation problems are rarely isolated words; keeping notes attached to the full textual moment preserves nuance.

### Language learners entering independent reading

- Who they are: learners moving from exercises and lessons into reading real books, stories, essays, and articles on their own.
- How they use NoteAnchor: they collect useful words, expressions, and phrases together with the original sentence while reading real texts.
- Why contextual notes help them: NoteAnchor can work like a contextual phrase notebook for language learners entering independent reading, helping them keep useful words and phrases together with the sentences that made them meaningful.

## Product insight

People learning to read independently often combine grammatical structure with linguistic intuition. They follow a hunch, then check meaning through the surrounding sentence and context. NoteAnchor supports that kind of reading by keeping notes connected to the exact phrase that sparked the question, idea, or recognition.

## Main use cases

- Review a text without editing the original.
- Collect research observations while reading source material.
- Mark revision ideas in a draft for later decision-making.
- Collect translation difficulties, tone questions, or stylistic options.
- Collect useful words and phrases together with the original sentence while entering independent reading.

## Current feature summary

- Local desktop app
- TXT support
- DOCX plain text support
- Contextual notes attached to exact text fragments
- All notes view for browsing and search
- Link again for reconnecting notes after text changes
- Export notes to a readable report
- Print report through a browser-printable saved report
- Help inside the app
- Original files are not modified
- Notes are stored beside the document

## Honest current limitations

- DOCX is opened as plain text.
- Document formatting is not preserved.
- Original documents are not edited inside NoteAnchor.
- Print report saves a browser-printable report rather than printing directly from NoteAnchor.

## Suggested public wording

NoteAnchor is a local desktop tool for active reading and contextual notes. Select an exact word, phrase, or sentence, attach a note to it, and keep that note connected to the original text while you read. Review everything later in one place, export a readable notes report, or save a print-ready report for your browser. The original document stays unchanged.
